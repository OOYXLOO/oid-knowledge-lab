Article fit

Proposed tutorial

The tutorial shows how a team can import generated evidence reports into Directus, review them through Data Studio, control publication with statuses and permissions, and expose only safe proof links to public readers. It uses OID Knowledge Lab as an implementation-backed sample while keeping raw private inputs and local-only crawl output out of the published package.

Reader questionReview answer
Why Directus?The workflow needs structured collections, relations, review statuses, permissions, API imports, and editorial visibility over generated evidence.
What is built?A review hub with evidence sources, review artifacts, remediation items, public proof links, hashes, record counts, and acceptance notes.
What stays out?Credentials, account exports, customer inventories, private contact exports, API tokens, raw third-party page mirrors, and local-only crawl output.

Directus model

Collections and workflow

Collection evidence_sources

Public URL, source type, boundary note, last checked time, and source notes for the generated evidence pipeline.

Collection review_artifacts

Artifact type, source relation, hash, record count, publishable flag, review status, public proof link, and acceptance note.

Collection remediation_items

Severity, status, related artifact, owner note, and safe next step for records that need human review before publication.
