Decision summary

Use when the assignment needs practical developer education

The sample set is strongest for articles where the writer needs to build or verify the example before explaining it. The public package combines a runnable Node.js project, generated reports, static proof pages, source links, and explicit boundaries around credentials, private inventories, copied third-party content, and account data.

Originality boundary

Reference samples, not syndicated final submissions

The linked drafts and briefs are proof of implementation depth and writing style. For an accepted assignment, the final article should be written as a fresh platform-specific draft using the publication's requested format, product surface, screenshots, and editorial style.

Public safety boundary

What stays out of public samples

The public pages do not include credentials, private customer inventories, private account exports, payment data, account-verification material, raw third-party page-body mirrors, or unpublished client information. They show original workflows, derived reports, and reusable documentation patterns.
