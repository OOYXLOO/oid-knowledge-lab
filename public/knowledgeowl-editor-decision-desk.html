Why this should be easy to evaluate

The pitch is not a broad AI article. It is a narrow documentation workflow for reviewing AI-assisted knowledge base updates before publication. The reader leaves with a small evidence-log template that can be used in a ticket, document, or review comment.

QuestionAnswer for the editor
Who is it for?Knowledge base owners, support leads, documentation writers, CX managers, and non-programmer reviewers.
What is new?It turns AI-assisted draft review into a visible decision trail: reader question, source facts, draft claims, checks, blockers, privacy boundary, and final decision.
Why now?Teams are already using AI to draft help content, but many review notes still hide the evidence behind publish decisions.
What is excluded?No private customer records, credentials, account screenshots, commercial terms, unpublished support transcripts, or copied article bodies.

I would like to propose a practical article for documentation and support teams: how to review AI-assisted knowledge base updates with a small evidence log. The article would show how to define the reader question, separate source facts from draft claims, record reviewer checks, mark blockers, protect private customer context, and end with a clear publish/revise/hold decision.
