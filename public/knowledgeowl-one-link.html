Reader promise

Make AI-assisted help articles easier to approve safely

The proposed article gives documentation and support teams a lightweight review workflow: define the reader question, separate source facts from draft claims, record reviewer checks, mark publication blockers, and keep sensitive customer or account data out of the handoff. It is designed to fit inside ordinary ticket comments, review notes, or internal handoffs instead of requiring a new engineering system.

Reader problemArticle answer
AI-assisted drafts can sound confident before claims are verified.Treat the draft as a review object, not a source of truth.
Support facts, SME comments, and draft wording get mixed together.Separate source facts from draft claims before publication.
Review notes can accidentally spread customer data.Use a privacy boundary that summarizes context without copying sensitive details.
Teams need a decision, not just more comments.End each log with publish, revise, hold for SME review, or do not publish.

Editorial boundary

Fresh article, safe proof

These links are review aids. A final article should be freshly written for KnowledgeOwl's audience and should not include private customer data, copied third-party article bodies, credentials, payment details, account-local screenshots, support-thread excerpts, or unpublished internal records.
