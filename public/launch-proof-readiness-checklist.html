Quick pass

Five questions a reviewer should not have to ask

If any answer is missing, the launch package needs tightening before it is shown to judges, sponsors, users, maintainers, or customers.

QuestionStrong answerWeak signal
What should I open first?One primary demo, README, or proof page is clearly marked.Several links compete for attention.
What works today?Working behavior is separated from mock data, dry runs, and future plans.The page implies production readiness without evidence.
Why does this matter?The audience, problem, and first use case are stated in plain language.The copy lists features but not the user's reason to care.
How can I verify it?Public links, logs, screenshots, manifests, tests, or reproducible steps are available.Proof only exists in private messages or local screenshots.
What is intentionally out of scope?Known limits are explicit and do not undermine the core promise.Limitations appear only after someone asks.

Safe intake

What to prepare before asking for a launch proof pass

Share public or sanitized materials only: repository link, demo URL, existing README, screenshots, short video draft, submission fields, and a short note about what reviewers should understand in the first minute. Remove credentials, API keys, private tokens, customer data, payment details, and unreleased confidential material.

Related service

When a focused sprint is enough

A launch proof sprint is useful when the prototype already exists, but the public story, README, demo path, screenshots, submission fields, or evidence boundary are scattered. It is not a replacement for building the whole product from scratch.
