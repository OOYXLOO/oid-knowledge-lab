Statement of work

What this sprint does

The sprint turns scattered prototype materials into a short, reviewable launch package. It clarifies what the project does, what proof exists, what the first-minute review path should be, and what should remain out of public claims until stronger evidence exists.

AreaIncluded work
PositioningTighten the headline, short description, audience, use case, and first action.
Proof pathChoose the first link reviewers should open, then order supporting evidence links.
Copy passRewrite or tighten README, landing page, submission fields, or launch notes.
Demo supportCreate a screenshot list, short video outline, or walkthrough sequence.
Risk boundarySeparate live proof, dry-run proof, generated content, mocks, and missing integrations.
Final handoffDeliver a checklist of what is ready, what is missing, and what to publish next.

Acceptance criteria

How to know the sprint is complete

CriterionEvidence
First-minute clarityA new reviewer can identify the project, audience, primary demo, and strongest claim without a call.
Proof orderingPublic links are ordered from strongest to weakest and unsupported claims are removed or qualified.
Copy readinessThe selected README, landing copy, submission fields, or launch notes are ready for owner review.
Publication boundaryPrivate credentials, customer data, unreleased confidential material, and unsupported claims are excluded.
Next actionThe owner has a short checklist for what to publish, record, test, or remove before launch.

Out of scope

What this sprint does not promise

This sprint does not build a complete product from scratch, guarantee awards or sales, fabricate metrics, bypass platform review, operate private accounts, or handle payment, tax, identity, or credential setup. It improves launch clarity and evidence quality for a prototype that already exists.
