First contact

Copyable short outreach

Use this when replying to a relevant public request, marketplace lead, or technical owner who already has an OID inventory problem.

Hello, I can help with a small first review of your OID inventory.

The safest starting point is a sanitized CSV with 20-200 rows and an oid column.
I will classify valid/invalid rows, map public evidence where available, separate owner-review questions, and return a Markdown summary plus an importable cleanup CSV.

No credentials, private account exports, customer names, or production hostnames are needed for the first pass.
Review scope: https://ooyxloo.github.io/oid-knowledge-lab/starter-scope.html

Qualification

Accept and reject rules

DecisionRuleReason
AcceptThe client can share a sanitized sample and wants classification, evidence, or cleanup planning.The first deliverable is bounded and verifiable.
AcceptThe request involves PKI policy OIDs, enterprise OIDs, MIB modules, or an internal registry.These match the tool's current proof surface.
RejectThe client requires private account login, production exports, credentials, or urgent live-system changes before scoping.That is not a safe first engagement.
RejectThe request is only generic SNMP monitoring setup with no OID evidence or registry cleanup component.That is a broader operations job, not this focused service.

Buyer checklist

What to prepare for the first review

  • A sanitized CSV, TSV, or text list with one OID per row.
  • Optional safe labels such as certificate profile, device family, MIB module, service, or internal registry id.
  • A short sentence describing the decision the team needs to make after the review.
  • No passwords, tokens, customer names, billing data, private hostnames, or private account exports.
