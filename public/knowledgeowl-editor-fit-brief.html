Reader promise

Give reviewers a simple evidence log before publishing AI-assisted help content

The article shows a small review pattern that can live inside an ordinary ticket, pull request, or internal note. A reviewer writes the reader question, lists source facts, separates draft claims from evidence, records checks, and ends with a decision: publish, revise, hold for subject-matter review, or do not publish.

Proposed article shape

Outline for the final article

  1. Start with the risk: confident AI drafts are not the same as reviewed help content.
  2. Define the reader question before editing the draft.
  3. Separate source facts from draft claims.
  4. Record reviewer checks in a short evidence log.
  5. Use a privacy boundary for customer and account context.
  6. End with a publish/revise/hold decision that future editors can understand.

Sample excerpt

Tone target

A good evidence log should feel like a helpful editorial checklist, not a compliance ceremony. The goal is to make the review decision visible: what the article is trying to answer, which facts support it, which claims still need checking, and what must stay out of the published page.

Publication boundary

Fresh writing, public-safe proof

The final article should be newly written for KnowledgeOwl. The linked project is only proof of preparation: sample templates, browser-only demos, and public writing examples. It does not include private customer data, account exports, credentials, support transcripts, payment information, or copied third-party article bodies.
