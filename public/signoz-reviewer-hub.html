Article fit

Proposed tutorial

The article shows how to instrument a small Node.js webhook worker with OpenTelemetry, export telemetry through OTLP, and use SigNoz-style traces, logs, and metrics to debug a failed background job. The stronger angle is not another generic instrumentation walkthrough; it is the practical handoff discipline that makes observability queries answer a precise question.

Reader questionReview answer
Why SigNoz?The workflow needs OpenTelemetry signals, trace-log correlation, metrics for retry and latency, and an investigation path that connects vague reports to concrete evidence.
What is built?A Node.js webhook worker example with spans, safe attributes, structured logs, retry metrics, and a copyable debugging handoff template.
What stays out?Authorization headers, API keys, raw cookies, private customer payloads, payment data, raw production logs, and private account identifiers.
