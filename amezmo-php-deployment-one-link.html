Article promise

Turn each PHP deployment into a reviewable handoff

The proposed article teaches a practical release checklist for PHP, Laravel, Symfony, and CMS-style applications: pre-deployment checks, migration evidence, worker and cache checks, health checks, rollback notes, and privacy boundaries.

Reader value

What the tutorial helps prevent

Release riskChecklist answer
Missing environment variablesRecord required keys as present without copying secret values.
Unsafe database migrationsRun dry-run or pretend checks and mark whether rollback is reversible.
Old background-worker codeMake queue restart and failed-job checks part of the release evidence.
Unclear rollback decisionWrite the previous revision, rollback owner, and decision deadline before deploy.

Tutorial structure

Draft outline

  1. Why PHP deployments fail in ordinary ways.
  2. Pre-deployment checks for dependencies, config, cache, and migrations.
  3. Deployment evidence for revision, target, migrations, workers, and health checks.
  4. Post-deployment checks for critical routes, logs, queues, and webhooks.
  5. Rollback evidence for revisions, database state, config, and cache.
  6. What never belongs in deployment notes: secrets, cookies, private customer data, and payment or identity screenshots.

Editorial boundary

Fresh article, safe proof

The public sample is a review aid. A final article should be freshly written for the target publication and should not include credentials, private customer exports, copied third-party article bodies, account screenshots, payment details, identity records, or unpublished client code.
