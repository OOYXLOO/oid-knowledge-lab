# KnowledgeOwl Editorial Fit Brief

## Reader Promise

The proposed article helps knowledge base owners, support leads, documentation writers, and non-programmer reviewers approve AI-assisted help content with a simple evidence log.

The workflow is intentionally lightweight. It can live inside an ordinary ticket, pull request, or internal review note:

1. State the reader question.
2. List source facts.
3. Separate draft claims from evidence.
4. Record reviewer checks.
5. Mark privacy boundaries and publication blockers.
6. End with a publish, revise, hold, or do-not-publish decision.

## Why This Fits KnowledgeOwl

Knowledge base teams need practical review habits, not another engineering system. The article is written for documentation and support teams who may use AI-assisted drafting but still need a visible decision trail before publishing.

The article avoids private customer records, credentials, account pages, screenshots, payment data, and copied support transcripts. All examples are synthetic and safe for public review.

## Proposed Article Outline

1. Confident AI drafts are not the same as reviewed help content.
2. Define the reader question before editing.
3. Separate source facts from draft claims.
4. Record reviewer checks in a short evidence log.
5. Use a privacy boundary for customer and account context.
6. End with a decision future editors can understand.

## Tone Target

A good evidence log should feel like a helpful editorial checklist, not a compliance ceremony. The goal is to make the review decision visible: what the article is trying to answer, which facts support it, which claims still need checking, and what must stay out of the published page.
