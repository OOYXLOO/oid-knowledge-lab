# KnowledgeOwl One-Link Review Packet

This packet is a compact first link for a KnowledgeOwl editor reviewing the article idea:

**How to Turn AI-Assisted Knowledge Base Updates into Evidence Logs**

## Reader Promise

The article gives documentation and support teams a small review workflow for AI-assisted knowledge base updates:

1. Start with the reader question and audience.
2. Separate source facts from draft claims.
3. Record reviewer checks and publication blockers.
4. Keep sensitive customer or account data out of review artifacts.
5. Decide whether to publish, revise, hold for SME review, or reject the draft.

The article is practical, documentation-focused, and written for teams that need reviewability without adding a heavy process.

## Proposed Article Structure

1. Why AI-assisted knowledge base updates need a visible review trail.
2. How to define the reader question and audience.
3. How to separate source facts from draft claims.
4. How to capture reviewer checks and publication blockers.
5. How to keep private data out of the handoff.
6. How to use the evidence log as an internal bridge, not a customer-facing artifact.

## Editorial Boundary

The linked materials are review aids and proof surfaces. A final article should be freshly written for KnowledgeOwl's audience and editorial process. It should not include private customer data, copied third-party article bodies, credentials, payment details, account-local screenshots, support-thread excerpts, or unpublished internal records.
