# KnowledgeOwl Documentation Readiness Pack

## Fit

This packet is for a KnowledgeOwl guest article or documentation-focused review.
It presents an implementation-backed article idea about maintaining trustworthy
knowledge base content when teams use AI-assisted drafting, generated reports,
and public evidence links.

## Proposed Article

Title:

```text
How to Keep AI-Assisted Knowledge Base Articles Reviewable with Evidence Logs
```

Reader:

```text
Documentation managers, support operations teams, and technical writers who need
to keep help-center articles accurate while using AI-assisted drafts.
```

Promise:

```text
The article shows a small, practical workflow for turning an AI-assisted draft
into a reviewable knowledge base article: define the user question, record source
boundaries, run a repeatable check, keep a short evidence log, and mark what
needs human review before publication.
```

## Short Pitch

I would like to propose a practical documentation article about keeping
AI-assisted knowledge base content reviewable. The piece would show a lightweight
evidence-log workflow for support and documentation teams: define the user
question, separate source facts from draft text, run repeatable checks where
possible, record known limitations, and mark the article for human review before
publication. I can support the article with public implementation examples from
OID Knowledge Lab, including generated reports, review pages, and a publish guard
that keeps private data and copied third-party page bodies out of public output.

## Outline

1. The real risk is not AI drafting; it is unreviewable AI drafting.
2. Start with the reader question and acceptance criteria.
3. Separate source facts, generated summaries, and human decisions.
4. Keep a compact evidence log for each important article update.
5. Use repeatable checks where possible, even if the article is not code-heavy.
6. Mark unknowns and review responsibilities before publication.
7. Keep private customer data and copied third-party content out of public proof.

## Publication Boundary

This packet is a review aid, not a final article. The final article should be
freshly written for KnowledgeOwl's audience and editorial process. It should not
include private customer content, credentials, copied full third-party article
bodies, account-local screenshots, or other sensitive account data.
