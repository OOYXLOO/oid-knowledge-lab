# Editor trust packet: implementation-backed technical writing

This packet is a short credibility layer for editors reviewing the OID Knowledge Lab writing samples. It is not a finished article draft and it is not a claim of assignment or acceptance by any publication.

## Editorial promise

I write practical developer tutorials from runnable implementations, not from abstract outlines alone. Each proposed article is backed by a public repository, reproducible commands, generated artifacts, and a clear publication boundary around credentials, private data, account pages, and copied third-party content.

## How I use AI assistance

AI assistance is used for drafting, refactoring, checklists, and review scaffolding. Final submission should still be adapted to the editor's assignment, voice, audience, examples, and originality rules. If a program disallows AI-generated drafts, the linked material should be treated as implementation notes and proof surfaces, not as a final article to paste unchanged.

## Why this helps the publication

- The editor can inspect working code before requesting a full draft.
- The article angle can be reshaped before writing begins.
- The final draft can include real commands, outputs, guardrails, and failure modes.
- The public package avoids credentials, private customer data, full copied third-party pages, and payment or identity information.
